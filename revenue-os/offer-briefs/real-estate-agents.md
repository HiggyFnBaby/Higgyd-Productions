# Offer Brief: Real Estate Agents (RealEstateOS Enterprise pilot)

**Agent:** offer-architect
**Input:** `../signal-reports/real-estate-agents.md`

**The single pain point this offer is built against:** #1 from the signal
report — solo agents lose real commission because they structurally can't
respond to new leads fast enough (average 15hr response time vs. a 5-minute
window that makes conversion 21x more likely; ~$427 lost per missed lead).
This is the entry with the strongest combination of frequency (7+
independent sources) and buyer fit (matches "solo agent" almost exactly).

**Why not #3 (kvCORE) as the pain point instead:** #3 has the single
strongest willingness-to-pay evidence in the report, but per the chain's own
rule ("pick the single strongest offer, don't split focus"), building
directly on #3 would mean competing head-on as a full kvCORE replacement —
a much bigger, more capital-intensive fight than the evidence currently
justifies. Instead, #3's evidence (agents already paying $500–$999/mo,
unhappy, contract-locked) is used below as *pricing and risk-reversal
reasoning* for the #1 offer — not as a second, competing offer.

---

## The promise

**Never lose another lead to slow response** — every new lead gets an
instant, automatic first response and follow-up sequence, so it doesn't go
cold in the hours before you can call back.

This is deliberately narrower than "a real estate CRM." It's the one thing
the signal report actually proves is painful and structural, not a feature
list.

## The buyer

Solo real estate agents and very small teams (2–5 agents) who:
- Currently juggle lead follow-up manually across showings, calls, and
  contracts (the signal report frames this as structural, not a motivation
  problem), and
- Are either priced into or burned by an expensive, contract-locked
  platform like kvCORE ($500–$999/mo) without feeling the lead-response
  problem is actually solved.

This is narrower than "RealEstateOS Enterprise" as a name implies — see the
flag at the bottom of this brief.

## The format

**Subscription, month-to-month, no annual contract.** This is a direct,
deliberate counter to the signal report's #3 finding that kvCORE "make[s]
you agree to a year contract before allowing you to see how bad their leads
are" — that specific complaint is the format decision. Monthly SaaS is also
the norm this buyer already pays in (report #2's $25–$200/mo CRM-alone
range), so there's no format-education needed.

## The price

**$129/month per agent.**

Reasoning, tied directly to the evidence (not picked by feel):
- The signal report puts the cost of a single missed lead at ~$427. At
  $129/month, the offer pays for itself by preventing roughly one missed
  lead every 3.3 months — and a solo agent working multiple leads a month
  should prevent that far more often than once per quarter if the tool
  works at all.
- It sits meaningfully below kvCORE's $500–$999/mo (report #3), so the
  pitch "less than a quarter of what you're already paying, with no
  contract" is a real, evidenced comparison, not an invented one.
- It sits at the upper end of the $25–$200/mo "CRM alone" band from report
  #2 — justified because this isn't a generic CRM, it's targeted
  specifically at the highest-evidenced pain point, not a bundle of
  unrelated features.

**Confidence flag:** the signal report itself notes its evidence is one
layer removed from primary sources (review-aggregator summaries, no direct
Reddit quotes) and recommends validating before finalizing. Treat $129 as a
reasoned starting hypothesis to test with real prospective buyers — not a
locked number.

## The guarantee / risk reversal

**No annual contract, cancel anytime** (the format decision above already
carries most of the risk-reversal weight — this directly removes the exact
complaint agents have about kvCORE). Additionally: **30-day
average-response-time guarantee** — if average lead response time hasn't
measurably dropped in the first 30 days, full refund. This ties the
guarantee to the actual measurable pain (response time), not a vague
satisfaction promise.

## What's explicitly NOT included

- **Not a full CRM replacement.** No transaction management, no MLS
  integration, no marketing website/IDX builder. This is the instant-
  response-and-follow-up layer only — it can sit alongside an agent's
  existing CRM rather than requiring them to rip it out.
- **Not a lead-generation tool.** This doesn't produce new leads (unlike
  kvCORE's PPC lead product, which report #3 shows agents are unhappy
  with anyway) — it only makes response to leads they already have faster.
- **Not built for large teams/brokerages yet.** The evidence base is about
  solo agents specifically; team/brokerage pricing and features are out of
  scope until that's separately validated.

"RealEstateOS Enterprise" as a name implies a broad, full-featured platform
for larger operations. The evidence actually supports a **narrow wedge
product for solo agents**, not an enterprise suite. Two honest options: (a)
rename/reposition this specific offer for the pilot (something narrower,
e.g. focused on "instant lead response"), keeping "RealEstateOS Enterprise"
as a possible later, bigger product once this wedge is proven, or (b)
keep the existing name and treat this as phase one of that larger vision.
Either is fine — this is a decision for you, not something to resolve by
picking a name myself.
